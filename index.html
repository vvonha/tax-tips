Common Deductions

Standard vs Itemized:
2026 Standard: $14,600 (single), $29,200 (married)
Only itemize if deductions exceed standard.
Mortgage interest - Deduct interest on up to $750K loan.
State/local taxes (SALT) - Up to $10,000 cap.
Charitable donations - Cash or items. Get receipts.
Medical expenses - If >7.5% of income.
Student loan interest - Up to $2,500.

Tax Credits (Better Than Deductions)

Earned Income Credit - Low-to-moderate income. Up to $7,430.
Child Tax Credit - $2,000 per qualifying child.
Education credits - American Opportunity ($2,500), Lifetime Learning ($2,000).
Saver's Credit - For retirement contributions. Up to $1,000.
EV Credit - Up to $7,500 for electric vehicles.

Reduce Taxable Income

Max out 401(k) - $23,000 limit (2026). Pre-tax.
Traditional IRA - $7,000. May be deductible.
HSA contributions - $4,150 single, $8,300 family. Triple tax benefit.
FSA - Use it or lose it. $3,200 for healthcare.

Self-Employed

Home office deduction - Dedicated space required.
Business expenses - Equipment, software, travel.
Self-employment tax deduction - Deduct half of SE tax.
SEP-IRA - Up to 25% of income, max $69,000.
Quarterly payments - Avoid penalties. Pay estimated taxes.
